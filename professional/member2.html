Professional Development

As I continue my journey as an Electronics Engineering student, I’ve learned that my growth is shaped not only by the lessons in class, but also by the person I am becoming. I may be quiet, friendly, serious, and sometimes funny, but deep inside, I am someone who thinks about my future and my family every time I face difficulty. That mindset keeps me moving forward, even when the course becomes overwhelming.

Being an independent learner teaches me to rely on my own strength, especially when solving problems, a part of engineering that I genuinely enjoy. Still, the path has not been easy. There were moments when the challenges almost made me give up, especially when it came to analyzing circuits. But these struggles taught me one of the biggest lessons in my life: never give up, even when things seem too heavy.

I acknowledge the areas I need to improve, such as time management, focus, and confidence. Yet, I also recognize that I work best under pressure, something that pushes me to accomplish tasks even in tough situations. Every small improvement reminds me that growth is a continuous process, not a race.
